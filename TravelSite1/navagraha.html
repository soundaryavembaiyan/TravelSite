MAHAMAGAKULAM

MAHAMAGAKULAM

Mahamaham Tank is a huge temple tank located in Kumbakonam. It is considered to be the foremost and one of the largest temple tanks in Tamil Nadu. The Masimaham festival held in the tank has 100,000 visitors and the once-in-12-year Mahamaham festival has close to 2 million visitors. Celebrated every 12 years.

CHAKARAPANI

CHAKARAPANI TEMPLE

Chakrapani Lord Vishnu and appears in the form of a discus or Chakra. Lord Vishnu sent Sudarshana Chakra to Patal Lok to kill the demon Jalandasura and have come out through river Cauvery. God Brahma, who was taking bath in the river, got impressed and installed Sudarshana Chakra here.

KASIVISHWANATHAR

KASIVISHWANATHAR TEMPLE

Kasi Viswanathar temple is in the heart of the city and is very close to the famous Mahamaham Tank. As per, the Lord Rama and Lakshmana prayed here to Lord Shiva and got a blessing to slay Ravana. You will also find 9 rivers known as Nava Kannigas. The Lord asked them to take bath in Mahamaham tank and pray to Lord Adi Kumbeshwara.

KUMBESHWARAR

KUMBESHWARAR TEMPLE

The Kumbeswarar Temple is an architectural marvel which reflects the typical Dravidian style. It is one large temple with 9 stories and a height of 128 ft. The unique feature of this temple is the 27-star sign and 12 zodiacs that are carved from a single large block of stone Other shrines of Ganapati, Muruga and Kiratamurti are also present in this temple.

NAGESHWARAR

NAGESHWARAR TEMPLE

This temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, the serpent king. It is believed that the temple got its name Nageswaran, as one serpent was found worshipping the lingam. temple is built in such a way that it allows sunlight to fall on the idol only during the months of April or May for three days therefore popularly known as Surya Kottam Temple.

RAMASWAMY

RAMASWAMY TEMPLE

The temple has some of the famous stone carvings and 219 wall paintings, which explain the series of incidents from the great epic Ramayana. This is the only temple where we can see idols of Rama, Sita and Lakshmana along with Bharatha and Shatrughna and Hanuman. Rama Navami is celebrated in a grand manner.

SARANGAPANI

SARANGAPANI TEMPLE

Sarangapani Temple is the biggest Vishnu temple in Kumbakonam. The deity is shown in a posture as if he is getting up from a reclining position. It is considered that Goddess Lakshmi arrived here from the Potramarai tank with thousand lotuses.

AIRAVATHESWARAR

AIRAVATHESWARAR TEMPLE

This temple is built in the 12th century by Rajaraja Chola II. Airavata, the white elephant of Indra and King of Death, Yama, worshipped Lord Siva in this temple.

UPPILIYAPPAN

UPPILIYAPPAN TEMPLE

In this temple, The Lord Vishnu is worshipped as Uppiliappan and his consort Lakshmi as Bhoomidevi. You can see the 8 feet tall idol of the Lord with Bhoomidevi on the right and Markandeya Maharishi on the left. The lord is also known as Lord Venkateshwara’s brother and so those devotees who are unable to visit Tirupati to fulfil their promises can go here.

Suryan(The Sun)


SURIYANAR KOVIL

Sun has its own value among the nine planets. It is a belief that person visits the temple that dedicated to the SUN GOD has good richness in worldly life. Simple to say that his/her life shines if prays the SUN GOD. Are you in need of happiness, wealth and prosperity in your life? Take steps to arrive to the temple exclusively dedicated to the SUN GOD.


Chandra(The Moon)


THINGALOOR

Chandra is a lunar god. True to say that the Moon is lord of plants and vegetation. Chandra is credit with young, energetic, beautiful, 2 armed and having lotus in hands. With the support of ten white horses, he rides his chariot across the sky by every night. Mondays are special days for Chandran. Thingalur is the sthalam for Chandran.


Angaraka(Sewai)


VAITHEESWARAN KOVIL

Mars is also called Mangala. Mars is also called Angaraka, meaning one who is red in colour. He is considered as the son of Bhumi, the earth goddess. He is the god of war. Generally, vaitheeswaran means ‘God of healing”. It is said that types of 4000+ diseases can be cured if prayed the Lord Vaitheeswaran. One of main highlights of the temple is the Lord faces the west.


Budhan(The Mercury)


TRIVENKADU

Budha is the name for the planet Mercury. He is the son of Chandra (Moon). He is also the god of merchandise. He is represented as being mild and having a greenish complexion. He is represented holding a shield, riding a winged lion.


Vyazhan(The Jupiter)


ALANGUDI

Jupiter is known as Brhaspati, meaning lord of devotion. He is called Deva-guru, meaning guru of the gods. He is personification of religion and piousness. He presides over Thursday. He holds a stick, lotus and beads in hands.


Sukran(The Venus)


KANJANOOR

Shukra means brightness and clearness. It is the name for the son of Bhrigu and son of the asuras. He presides over Friday. He is white in complexion. He holds stick, lotus and beads. “Kalathira Dosha Pariharam” for Lord Sukiran.


Sani(The Saturn)


THIRUNALLAR

Shani is embodied in the planet Saturn. He presides over the Saturday. The word shani also denotes the seventh day or Saturday in most Indian languages. It is to note that Shani is elder brother of Yama, the god of death according to the Hindu mythology. Shani is considered the god of justice.


Raghu


THIRUNAGESWARAM

Rahu is a severed head of an asura swallows the sun causing eclipses according to the Hindu tradition. He is depicted as a serpent with no body riding a chariot drawn by eight black horses. Rahu and Ketu are respectively called the north and the south lunar nodes.


Kethu


KEEZHAPERUPALLAM

Kethu represents karmic collections both spirituality and supernatural influences according to In Hindu astrology. Kethu is associated with the Meena Avatar (Fish Incarnation) of Vishnu. Ketu causes material loss in order to force more spiritual outlook in the person.
